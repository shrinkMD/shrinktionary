[CBT](/terms/cbt/) is the broad, most-researched family, built on changing unhelpful thoughts and behaviors through techniques like [cognitive restructuring](/terms/cognitive-restructuring/), [exposure therapy](/terms/exposure-therapy/), and, for OCD, [exposure and response prevention](/terms/exposure-and-response-prevention/). [DBT](/terms/dbt/) is a specialized branch for intense emotions, and [ACT](/terms/acceptance-and-commitment-therapy/) adds acceptance to the mix.

Other approaches in this cluster work differently. [EMDR](/terms/emdr/) is used for trauma, [interpersonal therapy](/terms/interpersonal-therapy/) focuses on relationships, [motivational interviewing](/terms/motivational-interviewing/) on ambivalence, and [psychodynamic therapy](/terms/psychodynamic-therapy/) on patterns rooted in the past.

Underneath the methods, a few words describe what makes therapy work at all: the [therapeutic alliance](/terms/therapeutic-alliance/), the trust between you and your clinician, and [transference](/terms/transference/), the feelings that surface in that relationship.
