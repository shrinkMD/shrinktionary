After a traumatic event, the [fight-or-flight](/terms/fight-or-flight/) response can stay switched on, which shows up as [hypervigilance](/terms/hypervigilance/), and the memory can return uninvited as a [flashback](/terms/flashback/). When alert gets too costly, the system sometimes does the opposite and disconnects, which is [dissociation](/terms/dissociation/), including the specific forms of [derealization](/terms/derealization/) and [depersonalization](/terms/depersonalization/).

The named conditions range from the short-term [acute stress disorder](/terms/acute-stress-disorder/) to [PTSD](/terms/ptsd/) and the more pervasive [complex PTSD](/terms/complex-ptsd/). [Avoidance](/terms/avoidance/) of reminders is a common thread.

Trauma-focused care has a strong evidence base. [EMDR](/terms/emdr/) is one of the approaches studied specifically for it.
