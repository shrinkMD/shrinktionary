The core of depression is often described less as sadness and more as flatness. [Anhedonia](/terms/anhedonia/), the loss of pleasure, is one of its most consistent features, alongside [fatigue](/terms/fatigue/), [psychomotor retardation](/terms/psychomotor-retardation/), and the looping thought patterns of [rumination](/terms/rumination/). The heavier words, [hopelessness](/terms/hopelessness/) and [worthlessness](/terms/worthlessness/), point to the parts of depression that most need attention.

The conditions here are the recognized forms: [major depressive disorder](/terms/major-depressive-disorder/), the longer-running [persistent depressive disorder](/terms/persistent-depressive-disorder/), and the context-specific [seasonal](/terms/seasonal-affective-disorder/) and [postpartum](/terms/postpartum-depression/) types.

On the treatment side, [behavioral activation](/terms/behavioral-activation/) is one of the most evidence-based behavioral approaches, and [SSRIs](/terms/ssri/) and [SNRIs](/terms/snri/) are common medications. If hopelessness or thoughts of death are present, that deserves attention today. In the United States, call or text 988.
