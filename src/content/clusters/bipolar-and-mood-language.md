What separates [bipolar disorder](/terms/bipolar-disorder/) from [depression](/terms/depression/) is the presence of highs. [Mania](/terms/mania/) is the full, often impairing version; [hypomania](/terms/hypomania/) is the milder one; and [cyclothymia](/terms/cyclothymia/) is a longer-running, lower-grade pattern of ups and downs. The highs come with their own words, like [grandiosity](/terms/grandiosity/) and [racing thoughts](/terms/racing-thoughts/).

This distinction is one of the most consequential in psychiatry, because the first-line treatment for bipolar disorder centers on [mood stabilizers](/terms/mood-stabilizer/) like [lithium](/terms/lithium/) rather than antidepressants alone. When mood symptoms overlap with psychosis, the picture can shade into [schizoaffective disorder](/terms/schizoaffective-disorder/).
