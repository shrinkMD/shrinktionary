Most psychiatric medications are described by class. [SSRIs](/terms/ssri/) and [SNRIs](/terms/snri/) are common [antidepressants](/terms/antidepressant/); [antipsychotics](/terms/antipsychotic/), [mood stabilizers](/terms/mood-stabilizer/), [stimulants](/terms/stimulant/), and [benzodiazepines](/terms/benzodiazepine/) each have their own uses. Knowing the class tells you more than knowing a single brand name.

A second set of words describes how medications behave and how they're managed: [half-life](/terms/half-life/), [titration](/terms/titration/), [augmentation](/terms/augmentation/), and what can happen if one is stopped too fast, called [discontinuation syndrome](/terms/discontinuation-syndrome/). The safety vocabulary includes the [black-box warning](/terms/black-box-warning/), [off-label](/terms/off-label/) use, and [serotonin syndrome](/terms/serotonin-syndrome/).

Nothing here is a recommendation to start, stop, or change a medication. Those decisions belong with a prescriber who knows your history.
